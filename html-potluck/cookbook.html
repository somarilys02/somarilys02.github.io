One Pot Cajan Sausage Pasta

Total Time: 30 Min Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • Smoked Sausage
  • Butter
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Chicken broth
  • Diced Tomatos
  • Milk
  • Cream Cheese
  • Pasta Of Choice
  • Chedder Cheese
  • Seasoning

Directions

  1. Place 1 spoon of butter in a deep frying pan. Here you can place sausage and begin frying.
  2. Once you see the sausage is about halfway through frying add the garlic and onion and fry with the sausage.
  3. Once you are finished frying add in the Diced tomatos, cream cheese and milk. Stir together.
  4. After all is nicely mixed add in your pasta and chicken broth just enough to cover pasta completly and cover to boil.
  5. Once the broth has desolved and pasta is soft, add in your chedder cheese an enjoy!

Baked Potatos

Total Time: 1 Hour Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • Potatos
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Onion Powder
  • Garlic Powder
  • Aluminum Foil
  • Toppings

Directions

  1. Its very importent to begin by washing your potatos. wash all you will be making by rinsing under hot water.
  2. After there clean, take a fork and poke holes throughout the entire potato.
  3. Take a pinch of oil, just enough to lightly coat the whole potato.
  4. season with your salt, pepper, garlic and onion.
  5. Repeat to however many you are preparing based on the amount of people eating.
  6. Wrap potatos in a sheet of aluminum foil.
  7. Place in oven for 30 min then flip for 30 more until soft.
  8. Let cool and cut open to add preferred toppings and enjoy!

Churros

Total Time: 20 Min Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Sugar
  • Cinnomon
  • salt
  • Water
  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Vanilla
  • Vegetable Oil

Directions

  1. Combine the ingredients for the dough in a medium saucepan. Let the mixture melt and come to a rolling boil, stirring frequently. Then, reduce heat to low. Add in flour and stir constantly with a wooden spoon until incorporated and the mixture forms a thick, smooth ball and appears to dry out.
  2. Immediately transfer dough to a large bowl and beat on medium speed with a hand mixer for a couple minutes to cool the mixture slightly.
  3. Add in eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition, until fully combined. It should appear smooth and creamy
  4. Spoon dough into a piping bag fitted with a large open star tip (we used an Ateco 829.)
  5. Add 2 inches of oil to a large skillet and heat until a temperature of 360 degrees F is reached. Holding the pastry tip close to the oil to prevent splashing, carefully pipe 3-inch strips of dough over the skillet, cutting the end with kitchen scissors, and drop into the hot oil. Repeat until churro bites fill skillet without over-crowding. Fry churros until golden brown, about 1 1/2 minutes per side, turning with tongs, as necessary.
  6. Transfer churros to a paper towel-lined baking sheet to drain for about 15 seconds.
  7. In a medium shallow bowl, whisk together cinnamon and sugar.
